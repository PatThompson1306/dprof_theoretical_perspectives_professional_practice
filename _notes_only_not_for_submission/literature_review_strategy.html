Inclusion & Exclusion Criteria

✓ Inclusion Criteria

IC1 Peer-reviewed journal articles, conference proceedings, and edited book chapters published 2018 to present
IC2 Grey literature from authoritative sources (NPCC, Home Office, CEOP, NIST, EU AI Office) published 2018 to present
IC3 Studies addressing NLP, machine learning, or computational linguistics applied to safeguarding, criminal justice, or related social contexts
IC4 Literature examining grooming, CSA, or online predatory behaviour with linguistic, behavioural, or empirical focus
IC5 Organisational, cultural, or governance studies relating to technology adoption within policing or analogous public sector contexts
IC6 English-language sources; non-English sources permitted where peer-reviewed translation or abstract provides sufficient evidence

✗ Exclusion Criteria

EC1 Sources published prior to 2018, except where foundational theoretical significance warrants inclusion with explicit justification
EC2 Non-peer-reviewed opinion pieces, blog posts, or media articles without evidential grounding, unless from authoritative professional bodies
EC3 Studies focused solely on adult sexual offending with no transferable applicability to child safeguarding contexts
EC4 Technical NLP literature without contextual application to safeguarding, criminal justice, or organisational deployment
EC5 Studies examining technology adoption in sectors with no structural or cultural comparability to policing
EC6 Duplicate publications; where multiple versions exist, the most recent peer-reviewed version is retained
